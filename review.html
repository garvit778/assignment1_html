A Good Girl's Guide to Murder

In "A Good Girl's Guide to Murder," seventeen-year-old Pippa Fitz-Amobi is obsessed with true crime. For her final year school project, Pip decides to investigate what really happened. She believes Sal is innocent and sets out to uncover the truth. With the help of Ravi, Pip digs into witness accounts, police reports, and hidden secrets. As Pip gets closer to the truth, she discovers that people in her town are hiding things. The deeper she investigates, the more danger she puts herself in. Can Pip expose the real killer and clear Sal's name?


The Silent Patient

The Silent Patient by Alex Michaelides is a psychological thriller with a twisty plot. The story revolves around Alicia Berenson, a talented painter who inexplicably shoots her husband Gabriel dead. Alicia refuses to speak after the crime, leaving everyone baffled about her motive. Enter Theo Faber, a psychotherapist who becomes fixated on getting Alicia to talk. He takes a job at the psychiatric institution where Alicia is held, determined to unravel the mystery behind her silence.


The Diary of a Young Girl

The Diary of a Young Girl, also known as Anne Frank's Diary, is a real-life account written by a teenager named Anne Frank.The Diary of a Young Girl offers a unique perspective on the war's horrors through the eyes of a teenager.It's a testament to the enduring power of hope and the human spirit in the face of unimaginable adversity.


The Fine Print

The Fine Print by Lauren Asher is a novel about billionaire romance with a grumpy-sunshine trope. Rowan Kane, known for his coldness and ruthless business tactics, inherits a condition in his grandfather's will. To claim his share of the family fortune, Rowan must become director of Dreamland, the whimsical theme park his family owns. He's opposed to the cheerful, over-the-top park, but there he meets Zahra, a passionate employee who embodies Dreamland's magic. As Rowan works with Zahra to improve the park, he confronts his own emotional baggage and discovers a softer side, leading to a romance that challenges both of their perspectives.


The Proposal

The Proposal, a one-act play by Anton Chekhov, dives into the world of wealthy landowners seeking to expand their estates through marriage. Ivan Lomov, a nervous landowner, visits his neighbor Stepan Chubukov to propose to Chubukov's daughter Natalya. Despite their wealth, all three characters are quick to argue over trivial matters, making the proposal process a hilarious and chaotic mess. This comedic play exposes the shallowness of these characters who value wealth and property over genuine connection.


Pride and Prejudice

Pride and Prejudice tells the story of Elizabeth Bennet, a witty and intelligent young woman from a middle-class family, navigating English society. Mr. Darcy, a wealthy and arrogant aristocrat, initially clashes with Elizabeth's pride and prejudices. Through misunderstandings and personal growth, both Elizabeth and Darcy must overcome their flaws to find love and happiness together.


Romeo and Juliet

In Verona, where the Montagues and Capulets are locked in an ancient feud, Romeo Montague and Juliet Capulet fall desperately in love despite their family ties. Their whirlwind romance blossoms into a secret marriage, but a chance encounter ends with violence and Romeo banished for killing Juliet's cousin. In a frantic attempt to reunite, Juliet fakes her death with the help of a friar. A twist of fate and miscommunication leads Romeo to believe Juliet is truly dead, and he takes his own life beside her. Awakening to find Romeo dead, Juliet takes her own life too, leaving the families devastated by the senseless loss and their age-old hatred.


The Kiss Quotient

The Kiss Quotient tells the story of Stella Lane, a math whiz with Asperger's who struggles with intimacy. Determined to improve her dating life, she hires a handsome escort named Michael to be her teacher. Their initial plan for a professional arrangement goes awry as they develop a connection that goes beyond the contract. As Stella and Michael navigate intimacy, emotional baggage, and their own vulnerabilities, they discover a love that challenges their preconceptions and blossoms in unexpected ways.


To all the Boys I've loved before

Lara Jean Covey writes heartfelt letters to her crushes but never sends them, using them instead to process her feelings. Disaster strikes when the letters mysteriously get mailed out, creating chaos in her life. To save face, she fakes a relationship with one of the boys, Peter Kavinsky. As they navigate this pretend romance, Lara Jean grapples with her actual feelings for Peter, her family dynamics, and the fallout from the unexpected deliveries.


Happy Place

Emily Henry's novel Happy Place follows Harriet and Wyn, a seemingly perfect couple on the rocks. After a recent break-up they've kept hidden, they reunite with their close-knit college friends at their annual summer getaway in Maine. To avoid ruining the trip, they pretend to still be engaged. As the week unfolds, navigating their charade and unresolved feelings, they confront what went wrong in their relationship and discover new paths for themselves, both individually and romantically.
